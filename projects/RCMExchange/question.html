Prior Authorization Delay for Chemotherapy Treatment with BCBS - Urgent Response Needed

Asked 2 hours ago Modified 1 hour ago Viewed 43 times
5

We're consistently experiencing delays with BlueCross authorization for urgent chemotherapy treatments. Average wait time is 5-7 days when it should be 24-48 hours.

Current process:

  • Submitting through BCBS provider portal
  • Including all required documentation (pathology reports, staging, treatment plan)
  • Marking as urgent/expedited
  • Following up daily via phone

Has anyone found a way to expedite these authorizations? Are there specific contacts or processes that have worked for others?

We solved this issue by implementing a three-pronged approach:

  1. Dedicated BCBS Contact: We established a relationship with a Provider Representative at BCBS who handles expedited reviews. Email them directly at the same time as portal submission.
  2. Pre-submission Checklist: We created a checklist of all possible documentation BCBS might request:
    • Latest progress notes
    • All relevant lab work
    • Staging documentation
    • Previous treatment history
    • Clinical literature supporting treatment plan
  3. Peer-to-Peer Process: Have the oncologist available for immediate peer-to-peer review during submission. We schedule this in advance.

This reduced our wait times to 24-36 hours consistently.

For BCBS urgent auth requests, we've had success with their Medical Director Hotline:

  1. Call their provider services line
  2. Request to speak with a Medical Director (have all clinical info ready)
  3. Mention it's for active cancer treatment requiring immediate authorization

Key point: Document everything. Get reference numbers for all calls and names of representatives. This creates a paper trail if needed for appeals.

Also, if the patient has a case manager assigned through BCBS (common for oncology), they can often expedite the process internally.
